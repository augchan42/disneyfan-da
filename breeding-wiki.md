# Breeding

*See also: Co-Hatching*

Breeding is a feature that allows the player to breed any two dragons together to get one offspring.

## Mechanics

From the My Dragons menu, the player can choose any dragon and click the "Breed" button.

Clicking the Breed button will cause a menu to open up and the player will be able to select another dragon or change the dragon they originally selected. The two dragons selected must be compatible with one another (see Rules & Requirements for more details). If all of these conditions are met, the player will be able to select the "Breed" button.

Once bred, one baby dragon will be created that takes on its parent's traits and it and its parents will enter a breeding cooldown.

## Rules & Requirements

Breeding requires two compatible dragons not on breeding cooldown aged adult or older, one male and the other female, and at least one available Lair slot. Breeding two dragons together will also warrant a breeding cost. After breeding, the pair will be unable to be bred again for a duration of time.

The player must have the world the dragon originates from to breed it. For example, to breed a Zeipera, the player must have the Fantasy world unlocked.

### Soul Bound

Non-world dragons including Daily Login, Event, Guild Wars, Season, and Shadow Altar dragons may only be bred with another of the same species. After being bred, the female parent of the offspring will become Soul Bound and be unable to be auctioned, bred, or traded. This also applies to Co-Hatching, however both parents will become Soul Bound. The offspring will always be female for dragons that are bred together.

When a player selects to breed a dragon able to become Soul Bound, a message will be brought up informing the player of the consequences of breeding species that can become Soul Bound.

### Breeding Cooldown

Once a dragon is bred, it will enter a breeding cooldown. The breeding cooldown varies between dragon species. The breeding cooldown of a dragon can be instantly skipped with a Breeding Cooldown Potion. Each time a dragon levels up, its breeding cooldown time will decrease.

Additionally, a dragon's breeding cooldown can be lowered or raised with either the Beautiful or Ugly genetic traits, respectively.

### Unlocking

Currently, only the five Shinrin world dragons (Karukiri, Mistrasune, Onagajin, Suiikipon, and Yaruakura) must have their breeding ability unlocked by the player. For the player to unlock breeding for each species, they must be the original owner to at least one dragon of that specific species that they leveled up to a given level (10 for Suiikipon, 20 for Yaruakura, 25 for Onagajin, and 35 for Karukiri and Mistrasune) to unlock breeding for that entire specific species.

This feature was implemented to "make the Shinrin dragons feel special and unique as opposed to them feeling like 'just' world dragons", as said by producer Blastii. "The way it has been, world dragons were just throw in-game and then mass bred within the same day.... it's our goal to make these dragons rarer especially since they are 'end/late game' dragons."

## Trait Inheritance

Baby dragons created both by breeding and Co-Hatching will inherit traits depending on their parents' traits. This includes the baby's colors, materials, element, pupil, personality, mutations, and traits.

### Colors

Each color slot on the offspring is taken from the range of colors between both of the parent's colors in that slot. See Colors for more details.

### Materials

Each material slot on the baby dragon will always be either one of the parents' materials for that slot, chosen at random.

### Element

Certain elements other than Fire, Water, and Grass can be obtained by breeding two specific elements together or via Elemental Potions. Breeding will have a 33% chance of producing a non-basic element based on what the elements of the two parents have. Otherwise, the baby dragon will inherit the element of either of its parents.

### Pupil

The pupil on a baby dragon will take after its parents' pupil type.

### Personality

*(See main article: Personalities)*

### Mutations

Breeding mutated dragons together will yield higher chances of getting a mutated dragon, compared to breeding two non-mutated dragons together. The more mutations the parents have, the more mutations the baby dragon is likely to have. Note it is impossible to get a full 5/5 mutated dragon from breeding, the highest being 4/5.

## Breeding Cost

A breeding cost, also known as a breeding fee, is a fee of coins that is placed when breeding any two dragons together. The breeding cost is dependent on the dragons' rarities. The dragon with the rarer rarity will always be prioritized over the more common dragon's rarity.

Dragons that are able to become Soul Bound (event, season, Guild War, Shadow Altar, and Daily Login) will have a breeding cost that is double the price of a world dragon's breeding cost.

*Note: Breeding costs written in italics are not yet featured in any obtainable dragons.*

| Rarity | Breeding Cost (Non-Soul Bound) | Breeding Cost (Soul Bound) |
|---|---|---|
| Common | 125 | 250 |
| Uncommon | 350 | 700 |
| Rare | 750 | 1,500 |
| Epic | 12,250 | 25,000 |
| Legendary | 37,000 | 75,000 |
| Relic | 500,000 | 1,000,000 |

## Trivia

- Unbreedable dragons will still show up in the breeding menu.
- The Atarix, Cocovira, Rocirus, Saurium, and the Suiikipon have the shortest breeding cooldowns.
- The Dratheros currently has the longest breeding cooldown.
- Soul Bound dragons of Legendary rarity currently have the most expensive breeding fee.
- Breeding costs and the Soul Bound feature were added on February 2nd, 2024.
- The Mistrasune was the first world dragon added to have a breeding cost of 12,500 coins due to it being of Epic rarity.
